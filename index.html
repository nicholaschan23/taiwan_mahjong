The Wall 牌牆 Pái Qiáng
Tiles are shuffled face-down into a square wall. Each player is dealt 16 tiles (dealer goes first). The remaining tiles form the draw wall.
Turn Flow 出牌 Chū Pái
On your turn: 摸牌 Mō Pái (draw 1 tile from the wall), then 打牌 Dǎ Pái (discard 1 tile face-up). Play proceeds counter-clockwise until someone wins or the wall is exhausted (流局 Liú Jú — draw game).

Claiming Priority 搶牌 Qiǎng Pái
胡 Hú (Win) > 槓 Gàng / 碰 Pèng > 吃 Chī. If multiple players want the same discard, higher priority wins. A win claim always takes precedence.
Winning 胡牌 Hú Pái
Complete a legal hand of 5 melds + 1 pair (17 tiles total). Win by claiming a discard or drawing the tile yourself. Declare "胡!" (Hú) to win.

Flowers 花牌 Huā Pái
Bonus tiles. When drawn, immediately set aside face-up and draw a replacement tile. They don't form melds but score bonus Tai (see Seat & Flower section below).

Core Mechanics & Payout

Winning Tile
You hold 16 tiles and win on the 17th tile — either drawn from the wall or claimed from a discard. How you get it determines who pays.
Payout Formula
Scoring is strictly linear — no exponential scaling. Total = Base + (Total Tai × Tai Value)
Win by Discard 放銃 Fàng Chòng
The player who discards the winning tile pays the full total alone. The other two players owe nothing.
Win by Self-Draw 自摸 Zì Mō
You draw the winning tile yourself. All three opponents each pay the full total, tripling your earnings versus a discard win.

Dealer Rules

Dealer Advantage 莊家 Zhuāng Jiā
The active dealer always adds +1 Tai to every hand. If a non-dealer wins, the dealer pays that extra Tai on top of the normal amount.
Dealer Streaks 拉莊 Lā Zhuāng
Every consecutive hand the dealer wins or draws, a streak bonus of 2n + 1 Tai applies (where n = streak number).
Streak 1 → +3 Tai Streak 2 → +5 Tai Streak 3 → +7 Tai

Wind Triplet Scoring
A triplet of your Seat Wind scores +1 Tai. A triplet of the current Round Wind also scores +1 Tai. If your Seat Wind equals the Round Wind, they stack as a Double Wind +2 Tai.
Flower & Season Scoring
Any flower tile drawn scores +1 Tai. Drawing your exact proper flower or season (matching seat number) also scores +1 Tai — some tables award +2 Tai for an exact match (house rule).

Violations & Penalties

Minimum Tai Rule
Most tables require a minimum Tai count to win (commonly 1 or 3 Tai). Winning below the minimum counts as a Fake Win, even if the hand structure is correct. Set your table's minimum in the Payout Calculator.
Fake Win 詐胡 Zhà Hú
Declaring "Hu!" with an illegal hand or below the minimum Tai.
Penalty
Round ends immediately. You pay all three opponents — each receives Base + a pre-agreed penalty Tai (typically 5, 10, or the table ceiling). Dealer forfeit the deal.
Short / Long Hand 相公 Xiāng Gōng
Holding fewer or more than 16 tiles before winning.
Penalty
Hand declared dead. You cannot win or call tiles this round, but must keep discarding. You still owe payment if someone else wins.
Self-Draw Etiquette
Keep the winning tile visibly separate from your hand until verified. If it touches your tiles, falls on exposed melds, or hits the floor before verification, the self-draw is invalidated — and in strict circles, treated as a Fake Win.
