Setup

Game Start 開局 Kāi Jú
The player physically sitting at the East side of the table is the first dealer.
The Wall 牌牆 Pái Qiáng
Tiles are shuffled face-down and arranged into a square wall, each side 18 tiles long and 2 tiles high. Each player's wall segment connects on the right end to the next player's wall; the left end stays open. The dealer rolls 3 dice. The total determines which player's wall to break, counting counter-clockwise from the dealer. That player counts that many stacks from the right end of their wall and splits it there. The side you counted from becomes the tail. The other side is the draw wall. Each player is dealt 16 tiles in batches of 4, starting with the dealer. If you start with any flowers, draw the replacement tiles in order from the dealer.

Gameplay

Turn Flow 出牌 Chū Pái
On your turn: 摸牌 Mō Pái (draw 1 tile from the wall), then 打牌 Dǎ Pái (discard 1 tile face-up). Play proceeds counter-clockwise until someone wins or the wall is exhausted (流局 Liú Jú, a draw game).

Dealer Rotation 過莊 Guò Zhuāng
You stay as dealer if you win or the hand ends in a draw. Otherwise, the next player counter-clockwise becomes the new dealer.

Scoring

Tai 台 Tái
Tai is the point unit in Taiwan Mahjong. You earn Tai from your hand pattern, flowers, wind bonuses, and how you win. Your total Tai determines how much you get paid. See hand patterns here.

Wind Rounds

Wind Rounds 圈風 Quān Fēng
A full game has 4 rounds. A round ends when all 4 players have been dealer at least once. Within a round, the dealer is always assigned the Round Wind; this can be tracked with the wind die. When a round ends, the Round Wind advances. A full game is at least 16 hands (4 rounds × 4 players).

Payout

Payout Formula
The payout per round is calculated using this formula: Total = Base + (Total Tai × Tai Value)
Automatically calculate payout here.
Win by Discard 放銃 Fàng Chòng
The player who discards your winning tile pays the full total alone. The other two players owe nothing.
Win by Self-Draw 自摸 Zì Mō
You draw the winning tile yourself. All three opponents each pay you the full total, tripling your earnings compared to a discard win.

Dealer Rules

Dealer Advantage 莊家 Zhuāng Jiā
If you're the dealer, you always add +1 Tai to every hand. If a non-dealer wins, the dealer pays that extra Tai on top of the normal amount.
Dealer Streaks 拉莊 Lā Zhuāng
Every consecutive hand the dealer wins or draws, a streak bonus of 2n + 1 Tai applies (where n = streak number).
Streak 1 → +3 Tai Streak 2 → +5 Tai Streak 3 → +7 Tai

Violations & Penalties

Minimum Tai Rule
Most tables require a minimum Tai count to win (commonly 1 or 3 Tai). Winning below the minimum counts as a Fake Win, even if the hand structure is correct. Set your table's minimum in the Payout Calculator.
Fake Win 詐胡 Zhà Hú
Declaring "Hu!" with an illegal hand or below the minimum Tai.
Penalty
Round ends immediately. You pay all three opponents, each receives Base + a pre-agreed penalty Tai (typically 5, 10, or the table ceiling). Dealer forfeits the deal.
Short / Long Hand 相公 Xiāng Gōng
Holding fewer or more than 16 tiles before winning.
Penalty
Hand declared dead. You cannot win or call tiles this round, but must keep discarding. You still owe payment if someone else wins.
Self-Draw Etiquette
Keep the winning tile visibly separate from your hand until verified. If it touches your tiles, falls on exposed melds, or hits the floor before verification, the self-draw is invalidated and in strict circles, treated as a Fake Win.

All 8 Flowers
八仙過海 · Bā Xiān Guò Hǎi
8 Tai
All 8 Flowers + Seasons. Instant win.
Seven Rob One
七搶一 · Qī Qiǎng Yī
8 Tai
Seven Flowers + Seasons and steal the 8th. Can happen two ways:
  • You have 7, and someone else draws the last one. You rob it, draw your replacement tile to reach 17 tiles, then count.
  • You have 6, another player has 1. You draw the last flower, set it aside, draw your replacement, then declare robbing the flower from the player with 1.
Flower Win Rules
花牌贏法
To complete a flower win (八仙過海 or 七搶一), you must finish your replacement draw first, then declare win. Flowers and seasons are not part of your 17-tile hand. If your hand can also legitimately win at that moment, those Tai count too.
